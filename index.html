Lakeside & Cabin Safety Protocol

Boating & Lake Safety

  • Life Jackets are Mandatory: Transport Canada-approved life jackets (PFDs) are situated in our exterior kit. It is the law and an absolute safety necessity for everyone to wear a properly tailored life jacket while on the open water.
  • Daylight Boundaries Only: Watercraft are exclusively cleared for daytime usage. No paddling after sunset or prior to sunrise—motorized boats cannot see you in the pitch-black environment.
  • Sober Operations: Do not use watercraft under the influence of alcohol or cannabis. Save the drinks for relaxing on the dock safely after all boating is finished.
  • Weather Management: If whitecaps form across the lake waves or thunder rolls into the valley, pull all boats safely to shore.
  • Lock Down Gear: Always return boats securely to their storage racks and snap down the SUP locks when finished to prevent gusts of wind from drifting them away.

Campfire & Stove Operations

  • Constant Presence: An adult (18+) must sit directly with a burning campfire or stove until the logs are completely burned out.
  • The Drown & Stir Rule: Never leave a fire to slowly smolder out. Deluge the coals with lake water, churn up the embers thoroughly with an iron poker or branch, and flood it a second time. It must be cold to the touch before sleeping.
  • Wind Variables: If high winds blow sparks or embers sideways toward nearby brush lines, extinguish the fire completely.
  • Buffer Space: Maintain a 3-foot clearance zone around the fire pit ring for chairs and blankets. Running or horseplay around the burning area is strictly prohibited.

Wilderness Elements

  • Rustic Footing: Cottage spaces are rustic. Watch out for variable sloping ground, exposed tree roots, and damp, mossy stone shorelines. Please wear solid outdoor footwear.
  • Pet Supervision: Keep pets under close watch. Do not permit them to roam onto neighboring properties, chase local wildlife, or swim out past safe visibility thresholds.
  • Secure Animal Trash: To protect local black bears, raccoons, and rodents, place all outdoor refuse containers directly inside our specialized Animal-Stopper protective bins. Never leave foodstuffs sitting on the open balcony decks.

Outdoor Living Guide

Biological Mosquito Dunk System

You will notice distinct buckets of standing water deployed across the property lines. These are treated with organic Mosquito Dunks—small beige donut rings that dissolve gradually in water. They emit a target-specific bacterium that naturally halts mosquito larvae growth. They are completely certified for watering troughs and are 100% harmless to drinking pets, birds, regional wildlife, foliage, lake fish, and helpful frogs or turtles.

🗑️ Trash & Sorting Protocol The Algonquin Highlands municipal site strictly mandates that local trash only be processed inside completely transparent plastic bags. Wrap all food waste and garbage inside clear bags and place them into the locked Animal-Stopper containers out on the front porch. Rinse all glass, metal, and plastic containers and empty them into the large blue porch recycling box. Flatten all corrugated boxes and place them on the sunroom table.
🔥 Fire Security If you utilized the indoor stove, verify that the heavy cast-iron door is locked tight and push the draft air damper entirely inside to close the vents. Check the outside fire pit to make sure the ash pile is completely wet and cold.
🧸 Lakeside Equipment Pack the dry balcony dining table cushions back inside their outdoor deck box. Return all kayaks, canoes, oars, life vests, and yard games back to their original storage zones.
